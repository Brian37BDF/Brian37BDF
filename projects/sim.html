Refreshed the online presence for a data-optimized television advertising company in NYC.
About this project:

While I was a designer at Ronik in Brooklyn, we worked with a groundbreaking TV ad-tech company to create a site that showcases their line of digital products and the team behind them. This project was one of my favorites as I had my hands on nearly every aspect of the project, from wireframes and mocks to illustrations and development.

Wireframes & Mockups:

My primary focus regarding the UI/UX of the Simulmedia redesign was two-fold. The first was creating a couple of pages that could break down Simulmedia’s product highlights and the second was creating an event center that could be used to both promote future events on social media and keep notes and videos of past ones.

The product offerings page wireframe, including a carousel of key features.
The final page, featuring an interactive diagram that I later developed to describe each element on mouseover.
A wireframe of an event page that already occured, but a highlight video is attached.
The final event page, with the CTA to RSVP for the event, or to join the mailing list.
Illustrations:

A secondary focus of mine on this project was the illustrations,including both diagrams and iconography. The diagrams I created were visual representations of several of the Simulmedia digital product offerings to break down each of their more complicated features.

An example of the VAMOS product diagram, with tooltips that describe each step on mouseover.
